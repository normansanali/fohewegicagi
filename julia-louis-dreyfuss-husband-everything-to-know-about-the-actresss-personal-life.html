Everything to Know About the Actress's Personal Life

Inside Julia Louis Dreyfus's enduring marriage with Brad Fall: The actress has been married twice!  

Widely regarded as one of the finest performers in the television business, Julia Louis Dreyfus is an actress and comedian. Julia shot to fame early in her career with SNL, where she was the youngest female cast member. Dreyfus's years-long stint on SNL catapulted her to become a household face but it was her role, Elaine Benes, on the renowned sitcom, Seinfeld, that made her a globally-recognized star. 

Born on January 13, 1961, in New York City, Julia started her comic journey with improvisational theatre groups in the early 80s. At 21, she had already made a notable name as one of the most promising female comics. Dreyfus has since conquered the television world by winning multiple Emmys and also made a stellar profile in films. Aside from her illustrious career, she is also known for having one of the most personal life.  

Inside Julia Louis Dreyfus's Enduring Marriage with Brad Hall!    

Not only has Jula Louis Dreyfus excelled in her professional field but also in her personal life. She found the love of her life, Brad Hall, early in her comic venture, and the two have remained tight ever since. Julia and Hall are one of the longest-running Hollywood couples. They met as aspiring comedians at Northwestern University in the early 80s and swiftly fell in love.   

The comedy troupe that Dreyfus joined early in her venture, Practical Theatre Company, was co-founded by Hall. The duo's career paths aligned after college as well when the two, along with the other two members of their troupe, joined the cast of SNL in 1982. While Brad's journey in the hit sketch comedy ended after two seasons, Dreyfus remained associated with it for a long time.  

The Seinfeld actress recalls the first impression she had of Brad. "He was gorgeous," the comedian said in a conversation, adding, "I remember thinking early on that this was the guy for me, but I didn’t dare tell anyone." The couple walked down the aisle on June 25, 1987, in Santa Barbara, California, and welcomed their first kid, Henry, in July 1992.   

Dreyfus gave birth to their second son, Charlie, in May 1997. Despite navigating a tough work-life balance, the multiple Emmy-winning actress and her husband continued to collaborate on several projects. The spouses joined forces in 2012 for their short film, Picture Paris, which was written and directed by Hall, and starred Julia.  

"The reality is that we really collaborate on everything," the actress said while defying the norms that personal and professional lives should be kept apart. The two also worked together on the television series Veep and Watching Elle. One might think that the couple's marriage, with their picture-perfect family, and successful work life, is a bed of roses, but the two have had their share of tribulations.    

Julia and Brad started their journey in a comedy troupe and later starred in SNL! 

The pair were put through the test of life when Dreyfus was diagnosed with breast cancer in 2017. Thankfully, for the actress and her family, the disease didn't end up straining their ties, and instead, only made them closer, and stronger. While crediting her husband and close friends for their constant support, Dreyfus explained how she felt strong following the cancer battle.  

The couple's collaborations go beyond their showbiz ventures. In 2018, the power couple used their celebrity platform to encourage citizens to vote ahead of the midterm election. The two created a PSA to advocate for better health care. As years go by, Dreyfus and Hall continue to gush about each other every chance they get.    

When asked about the secret to their long-lasting marriage, the Seinfeld actress quipped, "You gotta marry Brad Hall, that's what you gotta do." Hall's answer to the often-asked question is also as hilarious as Dreyfus's, if not more. "She's cheated so little. I mean, comparatively," Hall said jokingly before adding, "(Julia) never been interested in the trappings of Hollywood."  

Learn More About Julia Louis-Dreyfus's Husband and Their Kids!  

Dreyfus, 62, with her 10+ Emmy awards, and illustrious acting credits, is undoubtedly the better-known of the two. Dreyfus's husband of over thirty years, Hall, 65, might be known to some because of his wife's global stardom, but Brad is certainly a star in his own right. In fact, Hall boasts a multi-faceted career as he is a comedian, actor, filmmaker, producer, and writer.  

Brad spent his early career in the spotlight when he starred in SNL. He went on to have recurring moments in the limelight before settling for mostly behind-the-scenes jobs. Besides taking on small acting roles, Hall has produced, written, and directed a trove of hit shows, including The Single Guy, for which he won an Emmy nomination, Brooklyn Bridge, and more.  

Dreyfus and Hall's sons, Henry, and Charlie, are continuing their parents' legacy in their own ways as both of them have carved a career in the industry. The duo's oldest, Henry, is a budding musician. Since graduating from Wesleyan University, Henry has professionally forayed into music.   

Henry is also cementing a career in acting it seems as Julia recently revealed that her oldest "just finished shooting a series for Amazon." The Hall couple's youngest of the two, Charlie, is an A-lister-in-the-making. Since kicking off his journey in his famous mom's show, Veep, Charlie has built a notable portfolio, appearing in Single Drunk Female, Bel-Air, Drunk, Victor, Sex Lives of College Girls, and more.
