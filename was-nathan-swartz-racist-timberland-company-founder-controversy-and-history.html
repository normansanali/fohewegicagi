Was Nathan Swartz Racist? Timberland Company Founder Controversy And History

Nathan Swartz’s racist debate has been an ongoing discussion for a century. His remarks over the market target raised the racism concern over Timberland founder. 

Nathan Swartz was born in Odessa, Russian Empire, to an impoverished Jewish family in July 1902.

Nathan was the fourth generation of a family of shoemakers.

The family immigrated to the United States shortly before the First World War.

Swartz began his career as a shoe repair apprentice in New York.

Nathan left the company in 1968.

The Abington Shoe Company-a tiny New England shoemaker gave rise to the Timberland brand.

Abington Shoe created a waterproof, 6-inch Nubuck leather boot that could resist extreme weather in 1973 using an innovative molding technique.

The Timberland boot was the name given to it by the corporation.

Timberland boots accounted for 80 percent of Abington Shoe’s sales by the late 1970s.

As a result, the company’s name was changed to Timberland in 1978.
