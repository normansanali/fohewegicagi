A Look At Terry Bonchakas Grave 17yrs After His Death

Terry Bonchaka was unarguably the best Dancehall/Ragga artiste of his generation.

READ ALSO: 1Yr After She Was Introduced As Ebony’s Replacement, See Photos of Maureen And Why She’s Not Shinning

Unfortunately, his life was cut short in a ghastly car accident on October 29, 2003, while returning from a show at the University of Ghana, Legon. He died at a tender age of 21 at a time when he literally monopolized the airwaves and concerts across the length and breadth of the country.

His death shook the nation, leaving several fans including myself devastated. Terry Bonchaka was known for his unique sense of fashion and stage performance. He inspired a lot of artistes despite his young age. The likes of Shatta Wale, who is considered the biggest Dancehall artiste presently, often cite Terry Bonchaka as his influence.

Since he died, the SM boss has been visiting his grave to commemorate his death every year. On one of those occasions, he told Jessica Opare Saforo of Citi FM that if he had the powers of bringing back any dead musician, he would choose Terry Bonchaka. ‘Terry Bonchaka is one person I would love to bring back if I had the chance. I loved his style. From time to time, myself and boys visit his grave. We want Ghanaians to remember his legacy,’ he said.
